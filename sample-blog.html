10 fun and engaging classroom activities

Are you looking for some fun and interesting methods of learning which encourages the wholesome development of kids? Then, this corner will surely be a help for you!

As we are heading towards the most competitive era of education, incorporating different techniques of learning to traditional studies is a new necessity. Interactive classroom activities enhance confidence, creativity, skills, and encourage the holistic development of children.

In order to make learning more interactive and exciting, I have brought up a series of classroom activities that engage the students in academic learning, without them even realising!

I have also mentioned some special and unique approaches of gifting and rewards at the end of the blog which will make your interactive sessions worth a competitive fight among children. So, pull yourself together and keep your eyes and ears up until the end.

#1. Pictionary

This indoor classroom activity may be a blast from the past, but students still love it today.

How to play: Divide students into small teams. One student from each team is chosen to start and they must draw the subject-related picture you state, within a time period 30 seconds – 1 minute. Subject related guesses could be animals, characters from books you’ve read together, and other concepts you have discussed in class. The rest of the teams must then guess what he/she is drawing on the board. The first group to correctly guess the word wins. Keep the game going until every team has its turn or either you get out of topics.

Requirements: Board, Marker/Chalk, Subject-related topics, gifts.

Skills: Enhances thinking ability, analysis, Visualize understanding, teamwork.

No of members required: 4 (min) - 20+

#2. Dumb Charades

Both younger and older students will enjoy this indoor activity.

How to play: Divide the students into small groups. One member of each group (based on their turn) will step forward and act on the given word from the list without speaking. All the teams have to attempt to guess the right answer. Each correct answer will bring a winning point to that team and will stand a chance to act on the next word. Team with maximum points will taste the victory. Play in small groups so that each student gets more opportunities to participate.

Requirements: Charade cards or list based on things you have taught (Storybook names, personalities, character, games, etc). Board/Paper, Pen.

Skills: Active participation, Confidence, Visual Understanding, Representation, Teamwork, Communication.

No of members required: 4 (min) - 20+

#3. Blind Artist

A perfect learning game that tests a child’s ability to think, retain, and represent.

How to play: Pair the kids and make them sit opposite without facing each other. Give one kid a picture, cue, or image to describe it without revealing. The second kid will draw the picture with the help of the narration of his/her partner. This game is based on one’s imagination ability and is to see how accurate an image can be drawn when recreated without seeing.

Requirements: Images and pictures, White sheets, Pencil, and sketch pens.

Skills: Imagination, Communication, Narration ability, Understanding

No of members required: 4 (min) and more

#4. Jump The Line

A game to be played at the end of the chapter or book. This is an outstanding game to enhance learning, understanding, and memorizing a chapter and also includes a class revision or test.

How to play: Make a straight line using the chalk or the sticky tape on the ground. Mark one side of the line as True and the other as False. Ask the children to stand on the line. Ask a question – if the kids think it is true, they jump to the True side. Otherwise, They must jump to the false side. Kids who make mistakes will get no points and will be eliminated. (Elimination could be on no of wrong answers). Put a small gift for the student with the most right answers.

Pro Tip: Gifts will boost confidence among children to give a fight and will also act as a reward. Know more about the classroom activity gifts at the end of the blog

Requirements: Chalk or sticky tape, Questions, and Space

Skills: Attentiveness, Understanding, Retention ability, Confidence

No of members required: 10-15

#5. Bingo

Bingo is an incredibly fun game to play in a group, very easy to play, and can help rehearse anything from language vocabulary to math and historical facts. Everyone can play the game together, regardless of level.

How to play: Ask students to draw a 5 x 5 grid on their paper (grid dimensions could be chosen) Then let them select 5 words from the given list to write in their grid. The teacher should randomly select a word from the given list and describe it. Students must guess the word in order to cross it off on their present grid. Continue describing different words until one student successfully completes their grid in a row, column, or diagonally and shouts ‘bingo!’.

Requirements: Paper, Pen, words or numbers or subject-specific terms, board.

Skills: Attentiveness, Understanding, Guessing Ability.

No of members required: 2-10

#6. Chain Spelling

A game to enhance the observation and analysis skills over words. An interlinking of two different words due to their letters.

How to play: Divide students into small teams. One student from one team is chosen to start and they must write a 6 letter word. The second team has to take the last four or three letters of that word and form a new word. The third student does the same, and the chain continues until a student is unable to form a word. The team who fails to form a word or misspells it is out of the game. Don’t include unnecessary -ing, ion words which halt a fair play.

Requirements: Board, Marker/Chalk, A collection of Words.

Skills: Enhances thinking ability, analysis, observation, understanding, vocabulary.

No of members required: 4 (min) - 20+

#7. The Mime

A very fun and engaging game consisting of actions, words, and a lot of drama.

How to play: Write down a list of action words such as reading, gardening, dancing, walking the dog, dishwashing, eating, or singing on small pieces of paper. Put them in a box. Divide the class into two teams. The student has to enact that word while the other students try to guess what the word is. The team that guesses the word correctly gets a point and the team with the maximum points wins.

Requirements: Board, Marker/Chalk, A collection of Words.

Skills: Enhances thinking ability, analysis, communication, acting.

No of members required: 4 (min) - 20+

#8. Word Puzzles

Word Puzzle Game is an English word-spelling game where you make words out of given letters.

How to play: Both teams will be given a board with few letters. They have to make a word with given letters. The high-frequency words with maximum letters will get maximum points. The team with maximum points wins the game.

Pro Tip: Put some gifts for the winner. You can also add vocabulary cards to gifts. It will help them in learning new words and prepare them for the next activity round, More details mentioned at the end of the blog

Requirements: A board and key letters.

Skills: Vocabulary, Teamwork, Critical Thinking.

No of members required: 2 (min) - 20+

#9. Board Race

A fantastic game to enhance one’s vocabulary.

How to play: Divide the class into two teams. Give each team a pen or chalk. Draw a line in the middle of the board, dividing it into two parts, one each for each team. On each side of the board, write down a word. Following the relay method, each team must write words relevant to the theme of the given word. The team with the highest number of relevant words scores one point.

Requirements: Board, Marker/Chalk, A collection of keywords.

Skills: Enhances thinking ability, analysis, Vocabulary, memory, speed.

No of members required: 4 (min) - 20+

#10. Complete The Drawing

A fun team game of imagination and creativity!

How to play: Divide students into different groups. Give one drawing sheet to each group. Ask one kid of each team to draw something for one minute. As the timer rings, pass the drawing sheet to another team member to continue. This continues for 4-5 rounds including coloring. Combined artists could put forth a masterpiece.

Requirements: Drawing sheets, pencil, colors, paint.

Skills: Teamwork, creativity, imagination, art, and communication.

No of members required: minimum 4 to each team.
